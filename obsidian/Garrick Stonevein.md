## Backstory
Garrick grew up in a goliath clan that valued strength above all. He was strong, yes, but his real talent was understanding how things worked. While others competed in tests of might, he was taking apart mechanisms and putting them back together improved.

His clan considered him strange but useful when he rigged their mountain paths with warning systems and defensive traps. Everything changed when a vampire lord attacked their peaks at night. Physical strength meant little against creatures that could become mist or charm the mightiest warriors.

Garrick's explosive traps and alchemical fire were the only things that worked reliably. He managed to destroy several spawn and wound the vampire lord enough for it to retreat. His clan praised him as a hero, but Garrick knew they'd gotten lucky.

He left the mountains to study artifice and explosive warfare, determined to create weapons that could reliably kill vampires. When the Divina Virtus was being assembled, he immediately volunteered - where better to field test his anti-vampire weapons?

## Artificer Philosophy
"Vampires are strong, fast, magical, and immortal. That's four advantages. But they still obey physics. And physics includes 'things explode when I tell them to.' That's where we even the odds."
