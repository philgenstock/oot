# Kett Swiftwind
**Race:** Forest Gnome
**Role:** Scout, Navigator & Intelligence Specialist
**Age:** 94
**Height:** 3'2"

## Appearance
Wiry and weathered, with sun-bronzed skin and hair like dried grass kept in dozens of tiny braids decorated with feathers and small bones. Bright green eyes that miss nothing. Dresses in mottled browns and greens that seem to shift with his surroundings. His hands are permanently stained with ink from map-making.

## Personality Traits
- **Obsessively Observant:** Notices every detail, from the way someone walks to the smell of the wind
- **Paranoid (Justifiably):** Checks every corner, every shadow, every possibility
- **Speaks in Fragments:** Communicates in brief, staccato sentences when reporting - wastes no words
- **Nervous Energy:** Constantly moving, tapping, fidgeting when not scouting
- **Dark Humor:** Makes jokes about danger as a coping mechanism
- **Pack Rat:** Collects "useful" items that might be needed - has pouches upon pouches
- **Respectful of Nature:** Apologizes to trees he climbs, thanks animals for warnings

## Backstory
Kett spent seventy years as a wilderness guide and cartographer, happily mapping unexplored regions. During one expedition, his entire party was ambushed by vampires. He survived only because he was scouting ahead - returned to find his friends drained and turning.

He had to destroy them himself, then spent the next week tracking the vampire responsible. When he finally confronted it, he was hopelessly outmatched. Would have died if a paladin hadn't happened upon the fight.

That experience taught him two things: vampires are patient hunters who can be tracked, and working alone against them is suicide. When the Divina Virtus mission was announced, he knew his skills could save lives by keeping people out of vampire ambushes.

## Scouting Philosophy
"See everything. Assume nothing. Question everything. Trust your gut. Plan for the worst. Hope for the best. Always have an exit."

## Warning Signs (That Something's Wrong)
Kett has taught the crew to recognize danger signs:
- "Too quiet" - absence of normal animal sounds
- Unnatural mist in wrong conditions
- Plants dying in patches
- Strange animal behavior
- Unexplained disappearances of small wildlife
- Lingering smell of copper and rot
- Cold spots in warm weather
